Texts

  • Conflation of Technology and Language: A Cognitive Artifact

    Conflation is usually seen as an error. Here it is argued that our cognition works because of them, not despite of them.


  • The Affinity between Feedback Mechanism and Hermeneutical Circle

    An attempt to heal the Diltheyian wound, and bridge the gap between the natural sciences (Naturwissenschaften) and the human sciences (Geisteswissenschaften).


  • The Train is Coming for Us: The crushing power of fiction

    An exploration of the relationship between fiction, truth, and power. It is not always truth or falsity that matters, but what can be done.


  • The Social Function of Toy Models in Games of Science

    Though some philosophers have argued that scientific models are like props in games of make-believe, they have failed to underscore the importance of community in the constitution of those games. Here, this social aspect is analyzed.


  • The House of Futures: Cabinet of Speculative Curiosities

    We continuously generate scenarios about the future through forecasts, fiction, exhibitions, and other cultural and technical artifacts. In this article, the Futurium—an institution in Berlin dedicated to speculative futures—is used as a case study, examined by a gropup of experts, to explore which future narratives are chosen for display and which are disguised.
